What is a "Carbon Footprint"?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of carbon a persons emits through their lifetime by burning fossil fuels.

The term "carbon footprint" was created by 2 researchers: William E Rees, and Mathis Wackernagel. It was based on the "ecological footprint" concept, which is the impact of human activities on the environment around us.

They started research on carbon footprints because they saw that the quantity of waste that humanity was producing was greater than the rate in which ecosystems could replace it. They believed that tracking your emissions was a critical step to reducing them.

Year by year, carbon emissions have increased by a staggering amount. Doing your part is such an important step in helping the planet. Remember, a little goes a long way.
