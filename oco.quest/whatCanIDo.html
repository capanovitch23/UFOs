How To Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

1. Reducing computer energy usage.

Reducing energy consumption in your home is a large part of shrinking your carbon footprint. Shutting down your computer when you're not using it is a simple and easy way to decrease your energy usage. Computers are some of the largest energy users in our home, and powering it off when you're not using is not only beneficial to you, but also the Earth.

2. Unplug chargers that are not being used.

Be sure to unplug your chargers when they are not being used. Some chargers will still use a small amount of power when they're not being used. So whether you are using a phone charger, calculator charger, or even a car charger, you can unplug it, and help the Earth. Remember, a little goes a long way.

3. Wash your clothes in eco-friendly ways.

Washing your clothes correctly is another small way you are able to do your part. If you wash your clothes in cold water, it would reduce hot water usage, saving you money on your energy bill. You could also only wash your clothes when you have a full load of laundry. This will reduce the number of times you have to run the washer and dryer, meaning that you will have less energy being wasted.

4. Thaw your Foods in ways to prevent energy waste.

Another small way you can help, is to thaw your frozen foods in the fridge, instead of on a counter. This will help keep your fridge cold, because instead of wasting the coldness of your food, it will be put into your fridge, and keep your fridge cooler, while using less energy.

5. Find substitutes to driving.

When you have short trips to take, instead of driving, consider riding a bike, or even walking. It is not always necessary to drive a car everywhere you go. Not only will it add exercise to your schedule, but it will also help you keep your gas usage down.

6. Buy produce locally.

By buying more locally grown produce, you can lower your food miles. Meaning that less transport would be used to get your food from the farm, to your dinner table. Addiditonally, are you helping local businesses!

7. Go Paperless.

Turing a single tree into paper can produce more than 110 lbs of carbon into the air. Instead, make your documents digital. If your job allows you to receive information over the internet, instead of on paper, go for it.

8. Buy an electric car

This is more of an extreme solution, but if you are in need of a new car, consider purchasing an electric car! If an electric car is out of your budget, you could consider buying a hybrid to help reduce your carbon emissions.

9. Reuse shopping bags.

By using a reusable shopping bag, you won't have to keep using plastic bags. Each time you go shopping without a reusable bag, on average you tend to have about 5 new plastic bags, and using a reusable bag will be 5 less bags being thrown away into the environment.

10. Use bamboo products.

If you use a plastic toothbrush, and then throw it out, it will take close to 400 years for that toothbrush to decompose. If you switch to a bamboo toothbrush though, since it is plant based, it will decompose much faster, and leave minimal waste.
